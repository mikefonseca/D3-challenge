Extra, Extra! Health Risks Correlate with Age, Income

Health factors like smoking, obesity and quality access to medical care has been explored compared to human characteristics such as age and income (or lack thereof). Data and variables have been collected from the American Community Survey (ACS) and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) during the year of 2014 and further analyzed. Each circle signifies an American state.

Please see the interactive plot for various insights into the data. Choose an axis to examine each relationship.
A specific observation on the unavailability of healthcare as it relates to poverty, Texas shows the most people lacking healthcare, while Massachusetts has the highest rate and has been at the forefront of universal healthcare provisions due to policies from Mitt Romney and Elizabeth Warren.

Please hover over each state to see further characteristics.
